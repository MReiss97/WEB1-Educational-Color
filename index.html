Blue:

One of three primary colors in the RYB and RGB color models respectively. It lies between cyan and violet on the spectrum of visible light.

Purple:

Any variety of colors with hue between red and blue. It lies between blue and red on the spectrum of visible light.

Brown:

Of a color produced by mixing red, yellow, and blue as of dark wood or rich soil.

Red:

Of a color at the end of the spectrum next to orange and opposite violet.

Orange:

Of a color at the end of the spectrum next to orange and opposite violet.

Yellow:

Of the color between green and orange in the spectrum, a primary subtractive color complementary to blue.

Green:

Of the color between green and orange in the spectrum, a primary subtractive color complementary to blue.

White:

Of the color of milk or fresh snow, due to the reflection of most wavelengths of visible light.

Grey:

Of a color intermediate between black and white, as of ashes or lead.

Black:

Of the very darkest color owing to the absence of or complete absorption of light.
